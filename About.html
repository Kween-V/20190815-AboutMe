About Me

A little something about me

My name is Victoria, was born in 1998, on June 15th. I was born and raised in Hong Kong. I was sent to Canada when I had just turned 15, to study grade 10 in a private international school in Niagara, right by border. I lived on school residence thorughout my high school time, graduated in June 2016 with two business/ academic scholarships and a couple atheletic awards. Same year, moved on to study Management and Art double majors in University of Toronto, joint program with Sheridan College.

In my opinion, it is better to know a lot of things than to be really good at one thing but know nothing else, that might just be because of my personal interest. I enjoy learning new things and always looking for opportunity to learn new skills, gain new knowledge. When I was young, my weeks are always packed with classes and tutors, including figur skating, gymnastic, ballet, piano, violin, swimming, vlleyball, soccer, track, cross-country, badminton, etc. Although, I was lucky enough to be able to hang on to my favourite activity even in university - volleyball.

Passion

I went to an art and sport specialist school when I was in Hong Kong, from the moment you apply for the school, you have got to be either really good at art or really good at sport. I was an art student. Therefore, when I got onto the volleyball team in grade 7, I was not given as much attention as my teammates were getting, nor was I trained properly since they were all club player if not on the national team. Hence, when I got to Canada, I took my chance and self-taught through youtube videos and volleyball website, and my hard work paid off as I was able to take on the role as co-captain for my highschool team. Then been on the University of Toronto women volleyball d-league as a libero for over 3 years. The path to getting what you want is never easy.

When I was in grade 8-9, I had started an online shop on instagram, something like the dropshipping industry that is trending in recent society, even though I was very young, I managed the small business all by myself, from supplies ,to finance, to delivery, and to customer service, etc. Some might say I was silly, but not only I earned a couple extra bucks off that, most importantly, I took the risk to start the business when I still had to luxury to be able to throw in my all without affecting my life as I was still depending on my parents at that age. Unfortunatly, I had to shut down my store just after about 2 years because I came to Canada. In highschool while living on residence I was able to do some small business here and there such as selling chinese instant noodles (for example: I buy indomie by boxes, $12 for 30packs then I sell them for $10/pack). In university, there were friends that kept asking me to join them for business, but to me, having the right business partner is very important, and I would rather not have anything or even work alone than to have the not so perfect business partner. In the past year(2019), I have been planning and sourcing to start 2 of my major projects, one is my personal fashion brand, the other is a lash extension studio. *Fun Fact: My lash studio is opening in 2 weeks!* Let's all wish me a very good luck and come get lashes done by me :)
